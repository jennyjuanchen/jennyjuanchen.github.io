With the progress and development of modern science and technology, the experimental methods and research methods of biomedical field have undergone tremendous changes, exhibiting a "big data" trend, which involves a massive amount of omics data, literature data and clinical data. With the continuous accumulation of biological data and the progress of analytical methods, the understanding of the molecular mechanism of diseases is also increasing, which leads to the rise of "personalized" medicine . "Personalized" medicine is based on the personal genetic defects of patients, which involves the integration of different types of biomedical data, and utilizes comprehensive analysis to achieve a predictable, preventive, participatory, personalized disease prevention and treatment.
Deep knowledge of complex diseases requires a lot of knowledge. On the plus side, biomedical knowledge has been growing at an extremely fast pace, and the information comes from scientific literature, medicine and health blogs, social media, and so on. For example, nearly 2,000 articles are added to the largest biomedical literature database PubMed adds every day. However, most information is mainly presented in the form of unstructured texts. It is almost impossible to digest such a large amount of information (even for certain topics) within a reasonable amount of time. As a result, biomedical scientists need automated, semi-automated tools to help them acquire structured, machine-readable knowledge in a timely and efficient way to maintain a comprehensive understanding of the latest research.
In recent years, a number of curated databases have been developed to provide bio-molecular knowledge annotated by domain experts such as EntrezGene for genes, KEGG for pathways, DrugBank for drugs, and so on. The construction of those specialized databases requires experts in related fields to conduct biological review and biocuration of specific topics to represent biological information in a suitable way. For an example, the construction of Alzheimer's disease signaling pathways in the reconstruction of human metabolic maps, and so on. However, biological review and labeling are limited to awareness, financial support and other factors, which cause it far from being able to keep up with the pace of biological data. Thus, automated / semi-automated tools are required.
Text mining is useful for automated and systematic analyses of documents, and some techniques have been used in information retrieval, information extraction, knowledge base construction, and hypothesis generation. The main goal of text mining is to locate valuable information in the literature and express it in a structured way. There are already some successful applications, such as named entity recognition (NER), including genes / proteins, diseases, species, variants, compounds, and so on, and some more complex applications such as protein-protein (PPI) network mining, molecular event extraction (molecular event extraction) and so on. Text mining has shown great potential in promoting bio-curation as well as automatic extraction of biomedical knowledge.
In the past few decades, the number of biomedical literature has shown a rapid growth trend. MEDLINE and PubMed Central (PMC) are the most important biomedical literature databases. Among them, MEDLINE mainly contains the abstract information of the indexed literature, containing more than 21 million records; PMC mainly contains the full texts of the literature, containing over than 4 million records. It is estimated that the number of records indexed by MEDLINE and PMC is growing at an annual rate of about 4%. Any field experts are difficult to fully grasp the content of such a large number of documents.
The massive amount of literature poses a great challenge to the effective extraction of knowledge and information. Common tasks of text mining include information retrieval, named entity identification, relationship extraction and so on. Among them, the information acquisition is the process of obtaining the collection of corpus that is interested in. Named entity identification refers to the detection and identification of biomedical concepts (entities) appearing in text such as genes, proteins, drugs, disease names, and so on. Relational extraction refers to the interaction between the detection entities such as protein-protein interaction, gene regulation, the regulation of signal path and so on. Large-scale analyses of massive literatures are compute intensive. For example, it takes up to three months for BioContext to parse the entire MEDLINE and PMC Open Access literature on a local cluster with 100 concurrent processes. Therefore, how to use advanced computing technology to improve the efficiency of document mining is an important problem.

Tasks
1. Trial run
Install and configure the given components and the required software environment on Tianhe-2, then run the components correctly on a single node (using sample input and output).

2. Parallelization
1) On a single node, use OpenMP to achieve optimization, using 12 cores at least.
2) Use MPI to implement multi-node optimization and address the problem of load balancing found in the preliminary version.
3) Combine single-node OpenMP optimized work and multi-node MPI optimization to achieve an optimized implementation.

3. Test
Use the input sample to calculate the time required for each improvement and calculate the parallel efficiency.
