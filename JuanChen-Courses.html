Parallel Computational Thinking
Definition: Parallel Computational Thinking is the thought processes involved in formulating problems with diverse natural parallelism and their solutions with intrinsic parallelism so that the solutions are represented in a form that can be effectively carried out by information-processing concurrent agent.

Nowadays, parallel computing is everywhere, so parallel computational thinking is more and more important to help people to solve more complex problems in science computing applications.

Four-layer competencies students should have to reach the requirements for PCT.
It is our goal for students to have the following competencies. In other words, we think the students who have these abilities reach our requirement for parallel computational thinking.
* First, they should have the ability to abstract a solution as concurrent activities.
* Second, they should have the skills to identify opportunities for parallelization in task processing and data dependencies.
* Third, they should have the capacity to understand advantages of parallelism at different abstraction levels.
* Last, they have to have the ability to implement parallelism. For example, they can write programs using parallel programming techniques correctly.

Evaluation Rubric
We use a detailed course evaluation rubric (divided into three levels of achievement-sophisticated, competent and not yet competent) which can help students better understand what is expected of them through each stage of the process. The rubric addresses the student's project products, their presentation skills and their abilities to work well as a member of a team.
